# Indian Personal Finance & Spending Habits Analysis 📊

## 📌 Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive data-driven analysis of personal finance habits across different demographics in India. By leveraging **SQL** for data engineering and **Power BI** for interactive storytelling, the study explores the correlation between income levels, city tiers, and lifestyle expenses.

The objective is to identify spending leakages and evaluate savings performance against pre-defined financial goals.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack
- **Database:** PostgreSQL (Data Storage & Advanced Querying)
- **Data Transformation:** Power Query (M Language)
- **Visualization:** Power BI Desktop
- **Analytics:** DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)

## 🔍 Key Business Insights
- **Income Dynamics:** While Tier-1 cities offer competitive salaries, Tier-2 cities surprisingly show a more stable income-to-expense ratio.
- **The "Rent" Factor:** Rent remains the primary outflow, accounting for nearly **30% of total monthly expenses** across major urban centers.
- **Savings Performance:** On average, the national savings rate fluctuates between **25.6% to 32.6%**. A significant finding is that every analyzed segment successfully met their 'Desired Savings' targets.
- **Potential for Surplus:** Data reveals a potential further saving opportunity of **20% to 36%** by optimizing non-essential spending.

## 📈 Dashboard Highlights
The interactive dashboard consists of two primary views:
1. **Executive Overview:** High-level KPIs including Average Income (₹41.6K), Expenses (₹34.4K), and overall Savings Rate.
2. **Deep-Dive Analysis:** - **Waterfall Chart:** Tracks the transition from Gross Income to Surplus Savings.
   - **Spending Matrix:** Heatmap correlating Occupation with Expense Categories.
   - **Decomposition Tree:** Drill-down analysis of costs by City Tier and Age Groups.
